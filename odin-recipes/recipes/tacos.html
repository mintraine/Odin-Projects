Tacos

Description

A taco is a traditional Mexican dish consisting of a corn or wheat tortilla that is folded or rolled around a filling.

The fillings can vary widely, from seasoned meats like beef, pork, or fish, to beans, vegetables, and cheese.

This versatile food is then often garnished with toppings such as salsa, cilantro, guacamole, onions, or lime.

Ingredients

  • Tortillas: Corn or flour tortillas, served soft or hard-shelled.
  • Protein: Seasoned ground beef, shredded chicken, slow-cooked pork, grilled fish, or even beans and vegetables.
  • Seasonings: Taco seasoning packets or homemade blends with spices like chili powder, cumin, and paprika.
  • Toppings:
    • Vegetables: Shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, chopped onions, jalape√±os, and cilantro.
    • Cheeses: Shredded cheddar, Monterey Jack, or crumbled queso fresco.
    • Sauces & Salsas: Sour cream, guacamole, pico de gallo, or other favorite salsa varieties.

Steps

  1. Cook the taco meat (ground beef, chicken, or turkey) in a skillet over medium heat until fully cooked. Drain any excess fat.
  2. Add taco seasoning and a little water, then simmer for a few minutes until the meat is well coated and flavorful.
  3. Warm the taco shells or tortillas in the oven or on a skillet.
  4. Assemble the tacos: place meat in the shell, then add desired toppings such as shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, cheese, sour cream, and salsa.
  5. Serve immediately and enjoy!
